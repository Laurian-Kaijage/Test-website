Impact & Ground Work

Conservation outcomes, community engagement, and field work from Tanzania’s coastline

From the reef to the community — real outcomes on the Swahili Coast

Work on the Ground

My conservation work spans Tanzania’s coastline — from the coral reefs of Pemba and Zanzibar to the mangrove estuaries of the mainland. Every data point I collect represents a reef, a species, or a community that depends on a healthy ocean.

Key Impact Areas

🐠 Biodiversity Monitoring

Systematic ecological surveys tracking reef fish, invertebrates, corals, seagrass, and mangroves across multiple sites. Data feeds directly into conservation plans used by local government and NGO partners.

Outcomes: - Long-term baseline datasets established at 7+ coastal sites - Species richness trends documented across 3 ecosystem types - Monitoring protocols adopted by community rangers

🌿 Habitat Conservation

Supporting designation and management of locally managed marine areas (LMMAs) by equipping communities with data on habitat extent, health, and ecosystem services.

Outcomes: - Contributed data to support marine protected area (MPA) boundary proposals - Seagrass and mangrove extent mapped using GIS tools - Coral bleaching events documented and reported for early response

👥 Community Engagement

Working directly with fishing communities to co-design monitoring programs, build data literacy, and connect local ecological knowledge with scientific methods.

Outcomes: - Trained 12+ community data collectors across coastal villages - Co-facilitated multi-stakeholder conservation planning workshops - Built trust and long-term partnerships with fishing community leaders

📈 Data for Decision-Making

Transforming raw field data into reports, dashboards, and visualizations that influence conservation policy and attract research funding.

Outcomes: - Annual biodiversity reports produced for government and NGO stakeholders - Data pipelines built in R for automated reporting - Contributed datasets to regional biodiversity databases

Conservation Timeline

2019

Joined Mwambao Coastal Community Network

Began ecological monitoring fieldwork along Tanzania's northern coast, establishing baseline biodiversity data for key coastal sites.

2020

Community Training Programme Launched

Led the design and rollout of a community-based monitoring programme, training local fishers as citizen scientists to collect reef health data.

2021

First Documentary Participation

Featured in a documentary production covering marine conservation efforts on the Swahili Coast — bringing scientific field work to a wider audience.

2022

Regional Data Infrastructure Built

Developed R-based data pipelines and dashboards integrating monitoring data from multiple sites for annual reporting to government and NGO partners.

2023

MPA Support & GIS Mapping

Provided spatial analysis and ecological data to support marine protected area planning — including seagrass and mangrove extent mapping using QGIS and R.

2024 – Present

Ecological Data Manager

Leading data management strategy at Mwambao, overseeing biodiversity monitoring across 7+ sites, managing databases, and mentoring field teams.
