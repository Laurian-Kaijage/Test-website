Documentary Features

My field work and conservation expertise have been featured in documentary productions highlighting the challenges and progress of coastal conservation in Tanzania. These films bring the science and the human stories of ocean stewardship to audiences worldwide.

Documentary Feature 1

Year: 2021 | Region: Swahili Coast, Tanzania

Description: This documentary explores community-based marine conservation along Tanzania’s coast, featuring ecological monitoring fieldwork and interviews with fishing communities. The film captures the intersection of traditional fishing knowledge and modern conservation science.

My Role: Field researcher and technical consultant — guided film crew through reef survey methodology and facilitated community interview sessions.

Documentary Feature 2

Year: 2022 | Region: Pemba Island & Mainland Coast

Description: A closer look at the ecological health of Pemba Island’s coral reefs and the monitoring systems in place to track change. Features underwater footage of reef fish surveys, coral health assessments, and discussions of climate resilience.

My Role: Lead field researcher — coordinated all in-water survey activities and provided ecological commentary for the film’s scientific narrative.

Science Communication

Beyond documentary work, I communicate conservation science through:

📊 Data Reports

Annual and project-specific biodiversity reports translated from technical datasets into accessible findings for government, NGO, and community audiences.

🗣️ Workshops & Presentations

Presenting monitoring results at multi-stakeholder forums, including government planning sessions and conservation network convenings.

📱 Field Storytelling

Using photography and field notes to document and share conservation stories from monitoring sites — bridging the gap between fieldwork and public awareness.
